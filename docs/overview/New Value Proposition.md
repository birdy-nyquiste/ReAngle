## New Value Proposition

### Value Proposition of ReAngle

### Core Philosophy and Insight

**Product Philosophy**  
“Look at a mountain from the front, it's a ridge; from the side, a peak. Near, far, high, or low, no two views are identical.”

We have observed a fundamental phenomenon in the information age:  
What happens in the real world are **Events**. What people receive are always **Narratives** — versions that have been retold through different stances and styles.

The exact same objective event can be:

- Heroized  
- Moralized  
- Scandalized  
- Institutionalized  
- Ideologized  
- Entertained  

In other words: **Events are objective. Narratives are constructed.**

In the flood of the information explosion, countless manufactured narratives rush towards us every day. However, facing this “kaleidoscope” of information, most people are merely passive consumers; they rarely possess the tools to separate facts from viewpoints, or to actively reconstruct narratives from verified events.

ReAngle is built around a simple but powerful idea:  
**Before you re-angle a narrative, you must first de-angle the information.**

This means identifying:

- What actually happened (**Events**)  
- How the story is being told (**Angles**)  

Only after separating these two layers can a new narrative be responsibly constructed.  
This is the foundation on which ReAngle operates.

### Workflow

How do you take back the power of narrative when facing a trending event? ReAngle operates through a three-stage pipeline.

#### 1. Gather Inputs

Users provide multiple information sources related to an event. These may include:

- News articles  
- Research reports  
- Internal memos  
- Interview transcripts  
- Raw notes  

Inputs can be added through flexible methods:

- URLs  
- YouTube links  
- File uploads (PDF / DOCX / TXT)  
- Direct text paste  

Multiple sources can be added to an input queue before processing.

#### 2. DeAngle

Once inputs are gathered, ReAngle performs DeAngle processing. The system analyzes the text and separates two fundamental layers of information:

**Event**  
Objective descriptions of what actually happened.

**Angle**  
Narrative framing, interpretation, tone, or perspective applied by the original source.

The system then performs **Fact Verification** on the extracted Event list, classifying each event as:

- True  
- False  
- Unverifiable  

After this stage, users receive a structured breakdown:

- Verified Event List  
- Original Angle List  

This step transforms raw information into a structured understanding of the event landscape.

#### 3. ReAngle

With verified events separated from narrative framing, users can now construct a new narrative. Users define the desired perspective by specifying prompts such as:

- a new narrative stance  
- a perspective  
- a tone  
- which sources or angles to reference or ignore  

ReAngle then generates a new long-form article built on:

- Verified events  
- User-defined narrative angle  
- Coherent reasoning and structure  

The result is a narrative that is transparent in its foundation and intentional in its perspective.
